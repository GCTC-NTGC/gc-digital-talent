# AI Agents Guide

A guide to acceptable use of AI agents for this project.

Agentic AI is a powerful tool. Like any tool, it can used smartly and responsibly, or not. When using it for this project, follow the golden rule:

**You are responsible for your agent.**

AI cannot be held responsible. A human can. Anything you check into the repo, any comments left on github, are responsibility of **you, the human**, regardless of whether they were produced by AI.


With that in mind:
1. **Read everything you submit.** Do not commit *any* code you have not read. Do not accept a PR you have not read yourself.
2. **Understand everything you submit.** It is not enough to just read what the agent produced. If it suggests a solution you don't understand, great! That is a learning opportunity. Have it explain it to you. Then decide if its a good solution. Treat AI as a learning tool, not a reason to stop learning.
3. **Remain critical of the AI.** We know these agents suggest very good solutions at times, and at times not so good. We know they produce thorough code reviews which both miss things and focus on irrelevant details. You must continue to practice and excercise your own judgement.
4. **Help improve the tool.** As you correct your agents, have them record those learnings in AGENTS.md or skill files. Feel free to submit those changes in related PRs. In this way, help your fellow team members improve this shared tool.
